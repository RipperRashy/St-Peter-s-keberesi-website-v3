Life at St. Peter's Keberesi

At St. Peter's Keberesi, student life extends far beyond the classroom. We believe in the holistic formation of every young person — nurturing their intellectual curiosity, spiritual faith, social skills, and physical wellbeing through a rich array of co-curricular activities.

Students are encouraged to join at least one club or society and participate in sports, cultural events, and community service programmes throughout their time at school.

Clubs & Societies

✝

Christian Union (CU)

The largest society in the school, meeting every week for fellowship, Bible study, worship and spiritual mentorship.

🔬

Science Club

Students explore research projects, participate in county science congresses, and conduct experiments beyond the curriculum.

🎤

Debate & Drama Club

Develops public speaking, critical thinking and performance skills through inter-school debates and theatrical productions.

🎵

School Choir

Our award-winning choir participates in county and national music festivals, performing traditional and contemporary pieces.

🌿

Environmental Club

Champions sustainability through tree planting, recycling drives, and environmental education within the school and community.

📰

Writers' Club

Nurtures creative writing, journalism, and poetry. Members contribute to the school newsletter and inter-school competitions.

🤝

Young Leaders Society

Trains students in leadership, civic education, and community service to prepare them as responsible future citizens.

💻

ICT / Coding Club

Students learn basic programming, web design, and digital skills beyond the Computer Studies curriculum.

🌍

Social Welfare Society

Organises community outreach, charity fundraisers, and visits to local children's homes and elderly centres.

🎨

Art & Craft Club

Explores creative expression through drawing, painting, sculpture, and participation in national art competitions.

☪

Muslim Association

Provides a space for Muslim students to practise their faith, observe religious events, and grow spiritually.

📐

Mathematics Club

Runs problem-solving competitions, workshops, and prepares students for national maths olympiad events.

Boarding Life

Our boarding section provides a structured, safe and nurturing home away from home. Boarding students benefit from:

  • Supervised evening preps (prep time: 7pm – 9:30pm)
  • Three balanced meals per day provided
  • Dormitories with bed, locker, and washing facilities
  • 24-hour security and trained house matrons/masters
  • Weekend sports and recreational programmes
  • Spiritual life: morning and evening devotions
